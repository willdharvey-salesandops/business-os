## BODY

### How I got here

I spent years facilitating sales teams to get the best out of them: building them, leading them, fixing what wasn't working, and developing the people doing the work. I've sat in the rooms where deals were lost, where the founder was still the best salesperson on the team, and where growth stalled because everything depended on one person staying involved.

Eventually I moved into executive coaching and leadership development. I liked the work, but I kept running into the same thing: founders didn't just need someone to talk to. They needed someone to be alongside them, to help drive things when it got hard or lonely, and to actually build what was missing.

So that's what I do now. Less coach-on-a-pedestal, more trusted advisor who rolls up their sleeves.

---

### What I actually do

I work with small business founders, typically 5 to 20 people, who are profitable but stretched. The business works. It works because they make it work. And that's exactly the problem.

I help them:

- Build the sales structure so the business isn't dependent on them to close every deal
- Hire the right people and set them up to actually do the job
- Put systems and AI in place that genuinely save time, not add complexity
- Get clear on where their attention should actually go

The result, in most cases, is a founder who has more space in their week. To think strategically. To actually be present at home. To do the things outside of work that matter to them.

---

### How I work

I don't do the flying visit. I get into the business, understand what's actually going on, and work alongside you over months, not a one-off strategy day and a slide deck.

Most engagements start with a discovery conversation, then a proper strategy session. From there we work together weekly, building what needs to be built and fixing what's getting in the way.

It's part coaching, part consulting, part just getting things done together.

---

### A bit about me

I'm based in Surrey. Outside work, my honest ambition is pretty simple: quality time with family, a business that genuinely helps people, and more rounds of golf with friends and family. If I can find a way to pull all three together properly, and I'm working on it, that's the version of life I'm building towards.
